## Meeting Objective
Demonstrate the working Clinical Roundup product, validate the operational problem it solves now, and align on a phased path toward an AI-native CTMS capability.

## 30-Minute Flow

### Part 1 — Pitch: Team, Concept, Work Done (8–10 minutes)

#### Slide 1: Introduction
**Purpose:** Establish context fast.  
**Message:** We are presenting a production-capable Clinical Roundup application and a practical path to AI-native CTMS capability.

#### Slide 2: Team
**Purpose:** Establish credibility.  
**Include:**
- Product and business ownership
- Clinical operations expertise
- Technology and engineering depth
- AI and data architecture strength
- Compliance and governance readiness

**Message:** The team combines clinical workflow insight, software delivery, AI fluency, and enterprise execution discipline.

#### Slide 3: Problem
**Purpose:** Define the operational gap.  
**Message:** Trial operations are fragmented across spreadsheets, CTMS, EDC, eTMF, documents, email, and manual status updates. Leadership and study teams need faster, clearer visibility into status, risk, actions, and next steps.

#### Slide 4: Why Now
**Purpose:** Explain urgency and market timing.  
**Message:**
- Sponsors and CROs are under pressure to consolidate operational tooling and reduce cycle time.
- Enterprise-grade LLM platforms have reached the reliability threshold for governed, assistive clinical workflows.
- Incumbent CTMS AI features are largely bolt-ons, creating an opening for an AI-native entrant.

#### Slide 5: What We Built
**Purpose:** Position the current product accurately.  
**Message:** Clinical Roundup consolidates trial-relevant information into a structured operational command view for visibility, status review, tracking, and action prioritization.

**Key capabilities:**
- Clinical roundup view
- Structured study/patient/site information
- Operational status tracking
- Search and filtering
- Dashboard-style review
- Action-oriented clinical operations visibility

#### Slide 6: Why This Is Different From a CTMS
**Purpose:** Be explicit about scope and strategy.  
**Message:** Clinical Roundup is not a full CTMS today. It is a clinical operations intelligence layer that sits above EDC/CTMS/eTMF/Labs/Safety systems and accelerates decisions.

**Positioning:** Clinical Roundup is the wedge; AI-native CTMS is the phased target state.

### Part 2 — Demo: Clinical Roundup Product (10–12 minutes)

#### Demo Flow and Tightened Talk Track
1. **Open the application** — Show the landing view and orientation.
   - **Talk track:** This is Clinical Roundup. It gives study teams one operational view instead of cross-system hunting.
2. **Show the main roundup view** — Highlight organization of core information.
   - **Talk track:** The view structures status, issues, and context so teams can review quickly and act confidently.
3. **Show filtering/search/navigation** — Demonstrate speed of use.
   - **Talk track:** Users narrow instantly to what matters most.
4. **Show status/tracking elements** — Connect directly to oversight workflows.
   - **Talk track:** Teams see what is active, pending, delayed, or blocked and can prioritize follow-up.
5. **Show AI insertion points** — Point to near-term product evolution.
   - **Talk track:** AI can summarize records, detect missing data, classify issues, recommend actions, and draft leadership-ready updates.

### Part 3 — Path Forward and Decisions (8–10 minutes)

#### Slide 7: Evolution Path

| Stage | Focus | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Now | Clinical Roundup | Visibility and operational review |
| Next | CTMS foundation | Study, site, user, role, audit, data model |
| Then | CTMS workflows | Enrollment, monitoring, milestones, documents |
| Later | AI-native CTMS | Summaries, risk scoring, forecasting, natural-language queries |

#### Slide 8: How AI Helps
1. **Accelerate the build:** Requirements drafts, user stories, test cases, validation documentation, code scaffolding
2. **Enrich CTMS capability:** Study-status summaries, action extraction from notes, document classification, missing-info detection, operational briefings
3. **Differentiate the product:** Risk scoring, enrollment forecasting, milestone delay prediction, natural-language trial queries, executive intelligence

#### Slide 9: Medical-Grade AI / LLM Plan
**Message:** Use a model-agnostic AI architecture; avoid premature vendor lock-in.

**Evaluation areas:** Privacy and data protection, no customer-data training, audit logging, human review, source traceability, model version control, enterprise security, validation support

**Candidate platforms to evaluate:** Azure OpenAI, AWS Bedrock, Google Vertex AI / MedLM, OpenAI Enterprise / API, Anthropic Claude, and narrowly scoped open-source medical models where justified

**Positioning:** AI assists, summarizes, classifies, and recommends; regulated clinical and operational decisions remain human-approved.

#### Slide 10: Simple Timeline

| Timeline | Goal |
|---|---|
| 0–3 months | Confirm CTMS scope, gaps, architecture, and AI governance |
| 3–6 months | Build CTMS foundation: API, data model, RBAC, audit trail, study/site core |
| 6–9 months | Add MVP CTMS workflows: enrollment, milestones, monitoring, actions |
| 9–12 months | Add regulated workflows: e-signature, documents, validation package |
| 12–18 months | Enterprise pilot: integrations, SSO, AI summaries, risk scoring |

**Message:** Target a focused CTMS MVP in 6–9 months; target enterprise-grade, regulated capability in 12–18 months.

##### 12–18 Month Enterprise Pilot

- 20–30% reduction in milestone slippage in pilot studies

- Demonstrated AI-assist accuracy threshold approved by governance for initial production use cases

#### Risk & Mitigation

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Regulatory validation load (CSV / 21 CFR Part 11) | Slower release velocity and higher documentation burden | Stage validation deliverables from foundation phase, map traceability early, and align quality engineering with each release train |
| Incumbent switching costs | Longer enterprise sales cycle and integration resistance | Start as intelligence layer above existing stack, prove ROI quickly, and phase in system-of-record workflows |
| AI hallucination in regulated contexts | Decision-quality and compliance risk | Keep human-in-the-loop approvals, enforce source traceability, limit AI to assistive use cases first, and validate model outputs per workflow |
| Multi-tenant data isolation | Security, privacy, and trust risk | Enforce tenant-boundary controls, encryption at rest/in transit, environment segregation, and auditable access policies |
